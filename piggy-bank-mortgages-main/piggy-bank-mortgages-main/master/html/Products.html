First Time Buyer

We offer a range of mortgage products for first time buyers:

  • Fixed-rate mortgages - This means that the interest rate on your mortgage is fixed for a certain period of time. Usually 2-5 years.
  • Standard variable rate mortgages (SVRs) - This type of mortgage is usually set at the basic rate of interest. We may also choose to adjust the rate of interest charged.
  • Tracker mortgages - A tracker mortgage has a variable interest rate that is usually determined by the Bank of England's current base rate.

You class as a first time buyer if:

  • You have never owned a property used for residential purposes in the UK or even abroad.
  • You have only ever owned a property used for commerical purposed that does not have a residential space attached. (e.g. a shop with a flat upstairs).

The UK Government offers a range of schemes to help you get on the property ladder:

  • Lifetime ISA - You qualify for a Lifetime ISA if you are aged 18-40. You can save up to Â£4,000 per year and the government will add an additional 25% of your savings.
  • Right to buy - This scheme gives tenants who are in rental accommodation under a local council or housing association the opportunity to buy ther house they currently live in.
  • First Homes scheme - You qualify for this sheme if you are over 18. It offers new-build houses at a discounted rate compared to the market value.
  • Shared ownership - This gives you the opportunity to co-own with a landlord.

Buy to Let

Buy to let mortgages are a way for you move into the rental market.

How it works:

  • Deposit - The deposit required is usually higher than a typical mortgage deposit. This is usually between 20-40% of the value of the property.
  • Interest-only payments - We will expect you to pay only the interest each month, not the full amount.
  • Full amount payment - Once the term of the mortgage ends we will expect you to pay the full amount of the mortgage value owed to us.

Are you eligible:

  • Age - We will require you to be above 21 to apply for a Buy to let mortgage.
  • Deposit - You will need between 20-40% of the value of the property as a deposit.
  • Income - You will need to be earning above Â£25,000 to apply for a Buy to let mortgage.
  • Previous Borrowing - We will need to check your credit/borrowing history to see how reliable you are.

Re-mortgage

By remortgaging with us it means you want to switch your current mortgage to a deal offered by us.

Why do it:

  • Switch to a better deal.
  • Pay off the value of the mortgage sooner.
  • Release equity on your property.
  • Consolidate debt to reduce the value of your monthly outgoings.

How to do it:

  • Apply for your chosen mortgage with us. We will need to check things like your ID, payslips, and bank statements to work out your eligibility.
  • We will need to assess that you can afford the mortgage. We will carry out a credit check and valuation of your current property.
  • Once all checks are complete we may make you an offer.
